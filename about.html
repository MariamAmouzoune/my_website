Biography

Who I Am

I am a PhD researcher with deep expertise in plant pathology, genomics, and quantitative genetics. My doctoral work at Ibn Tofail University — conducted in collaboration with ICARDA — focused on unraveling the genetic architecture of barley leaf rust resistance (Puccinia hordei) using genome-wide association studies (GWAS).

Over seven years, I worked with international research teams to evaluate hundreds of barley accessions from wild and cultivated germplasm, combining field and greenhouse phenotyping with high-density SNP marker data to identify novel resistance loci. I am experienced in processing large-scale NGS datasets using R, Python, and Linux, and I have mentored BSc and MSc students in bioinformatics and population genetics at ICARDA Rabat.

I am now actively seeking opportunities in plant genomics, bioinformatics, and cereal breeding programs, with openness to international and remote roles. I am fluent in Tamazight, Arabic, French, and English.
